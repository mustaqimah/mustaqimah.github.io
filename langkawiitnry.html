Day 1:Arrival in Langkawi, Mahsuri's Tomb, Eagle Square & Kuah Town

Upon arrival at the airport or jetty, take the rent car and start your journey. If you flew into Langkawi, dive straight into some history at Mahsuriï¿½s tomb. It is only 15 minutes away by car. Familiarize yourself with the famous legend of Princess Mahsuri and the curse she cast upon the island. Here, you can also take a close look at batik painting and traditional dances.

Mahsuri Tomb, Pulau Langkawi

Then spend some time at the Eagle Square, a significant landmark of Langkawi. Eagle Square is home to the iconic Eagle statue of Langkawi Island so obviously, it is a photo opportunity that must not be missed. The viewing platform also allows you a panoramic view of the ocean and the nearby coast of the outlying islands.Take photos with the iconic eagle statue and enjoy duty free shopping around the area. You can buy things like chocolate, clothes and fragrances at cheaper than usual prices.

Eagle Square

Don't spend too much time here though because next, we'll be rounding up our trip with a visit to Kuah Town, just a mere 3km away. It is a good place for lunch as there are many restaurants and food courts there which sell authentic Malaysian cuisine. But more importantly, most of the best duty free shops on the island are located there. At night, visit one of the seafood restaurants in Kuah town or Cenang for dinner.

Kuah Town

Day 2:Cable Car, Sky Bridge, Oriental Village, 3D Art Museum & Underwater World

Start the day nice and early and visit the Langkawi Sky Bridge before the crowds arrive. Take the cable car up Mount Machinchang. It is the island's second highest peak. You can see as far as Thailand on clear days from the cable car as it ascends more than 700m above sea level. At the top station, walk on the Sky Bridge. Brace yourself for parts of the bridge that has glass-bottom slabs. Immerse yourself in the 360-degree view of the island's sea, sky and beyond.

Cable Car, Pulau Langkawi
Sky Bridge, Pulau Langkawi

Ride the cable car back down to the base of the peak, enjoy a few local Malay-style street food snacks at the many stalls found by the entrance. Spend the rest of the day within the Oriental Village. One highlight is the 3D art museum, the biggest in Malaysia. You can easily spend an hour walking through taking lots of photos in the 3d art.

Oriental Village, Pulau Langkawi
3D Art Museum, Pulau Langkawi

After that, proceed to the nearby Underwater World, the largest marine and fresh water aquarium in South East Asia. A good place to get close with the marine life without having to get wet.

Underwater World, Pulau Langkawi

But if you still have some energy to spare and time, head back up Mount Machinchang for another must see. Telaga Tujuh or Seven Wells waterfall was named after the seven natural pools formed by the flowing water. Hike to the higher wells for stronger currents and a more enjoyable experience. Look for something to eat around the area. Just 5 minutes away from the waterfalls is Panh-Thai Restaurant. It offers great food and amazing views, although at a higher price point.

Air Terjun Telaga Tujuh

Day 3:Island Hopping; Cenang Beach; Departure

As this is a short 3 days visit in Langkawi, it is recommended to leave this beautiful island in late afternoon or evening. In the morning, join a island hopping tour to visit the popular Dayang Bunting Island, Singa Besar Island and Beras Basah Island. Each island has their unique features and be sure to find out from the guide during the tour. Island hopping tour in Langkawi will normally takes 3-4 hours.

Tasik Dayang Bunting, Pulau Langkawi

By now, it should be in the noon time, proceed to the nearby Pantai Cenang (Cenang beach) for lunch. After lunch, visit the popular Cenang beach for some exciting water activities. If water activity is not something you like, perhaps, take a stroll by the beach or take a sit under the casuarina trees to enjoy the laid back beach atmosphere could be a good alternative.

Cenang Beach, Pulau Langkawi

In the everning, proceed to Kuah jetty to catch the last ferry back to the mainland or catch your flight in the Langkawi International Airport to continue your journey or back home.
